Introduction 

Ja Morant is a professional basketball player who represents the Memphis Grizzlies in the NBA.

Before that, he was a standout collegiate athlete at Murray State University, where, in his sophomore year, he was named to the first team of All-Americans.

After winning the 2018-19 Ohio Valley Conference Player of the Year award and finishing first in the NCAA in assists, he’s one of the youngest and most-watched NBA players in history.

Early Life 

Temetrius Jamel Morant was born August 10, 1999, in Dalzell, South Carolina, United States.

Ja is Tee’s and Jamie’s son, with his mother being a point guard in high school before switching to softball in college.

His father had previously played basketball at Claflin University and Teniya and his younger sister and brother Tee are basketball players as well.

He later played for the South Carolina Hornets in the Amateur Athletic Union and attended Crestwood High School, playing basketball while there.

By the time he left, he had 1,679 points, making him the school’s all-time leading scorer and, in his final two high school seasons, received 3A All-State recognition in South Carolina.

Morant was discovered by Murray State University’s basketball program by chance in July 2016 after assistant coach James Kane noticed him playing a game at a camp’s auxiliary gym.

He recorded the first double-double of his career the following month in a defeat to Saint Louis University.

Shortly after, Morant defeated Eastern Illinois while recording his first triple-double of his career and later assisted Murray State in making it to the NCAA Tournament.

Career 

He assisted Murray State in making it to the NCAA Tournament, where they ultimately fell to Florida State in the second round.

It was then that Morant made the decision to enter the NBA draft rather than finish his remaining two collegiate seasons as a result.

The Memphis Grizzlies selected Morant as the second overall choice in the 2019 NBA Draft and played his first game in an October loss to the Miami Heat, 101-120.

The following month, in a 117-115 victory over the Charlotte Hornets, he performed better and recorded his first double-double in the NBA – also making the game-winning layup.

With an average of 7.1 assists per game in his first NBA season, he stood out among all rookies, with 17.8 points per game and 1,138 total points.

He helped guide the Grizzlies to a 117-112 overtime victory over the Golden State Warriors in the spring of the following year, securing a postseason berth.

Morant helped defeat the top-seeded Utah Jazz in his NBA playoff debut, where the Grizzlies ultimately fell 129-141 despite his 47 points and seven assists in Game 2.

He joins other top players like Mike Conley, Kyle Lowry, Ish Smith, and Allen Iverson who played for the Grizzlies.

In February 2021, he tied his 44-point career-best before surpassing it by six points a week later and did so by breaking the Grizzlies player record for most points scored in a single game.

Just two days later, Morant struck again, setting the record with 52 points in a victory over the San Antonio Spurs.
