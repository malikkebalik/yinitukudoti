Who was Erich Zakowski?

Erich Zakowski, born on November 25, 1933, in East Prussia, Germany, was not only a master mechanic but also a visionary figure in the world of motorsport. His journey in the automotive world began after World War II when he, along with his family, had to flee from Prussia. They faced numerous challenges before finally settling in Niederzissen.

It was in Niederzissen that Zakowski’s passion for automobiles took root. He honed his skills as an auto mechanic and embarked on his racing endeavors by establishing his own garage. Zakowski’s determination and innovative spirit propelled him to success, and he soon became a prominent figure in the motorsport community.

Throughout his career, Zakowski’s contributions to the world of motorsport were unparalleled. His fast Ford Escort and Ford Capri Turbo secured multiple championship titles, surpassing any other DRM team. His influence extended beyond racing accomplishments, as he established the renowned Zakspeed racing team, which became a force to be reckoned with in various racing series.

Establishing Zakspeed and Racing Success

In 1968, Erich Zakowski took a significant step by establishing his own garage in Niederzissen, which would become the operational base for the Zakspeed racing team. Under the banner of “Zakowski Niederzissen tuning,” he embarked on his racing endeavors, starting with a Ford Escort at the Eifel race on the challenging Nürburgring circuit.

The 1970s and 1980s marked an era of remarkable achievements for Zakspeed under Zakowski’s visionary leadership. The team firmly established itself in various racing series, with a significant presence in sports car racing, further enhancing its reputation in the motorsport community.

One of the most significant milestones came in 1985 when Zakspeed made its foray into the prestigious Formula 1 series. This move was a testament to Zakowski’s ambition and commitment to pushing the boundaries of motorsport excellence. Despite eventually stepping back from the direct leadership of the team, Zakowski’s legacy continued through his son, Peter, who ensured that the family’s dedication to motorsport thrived.

Retirement and Family Succession

In the later years of his life, Erich Zakowski gradually distanced himself from his professional endeavors. After a period in the Canary Islands, he returned to Balkhausen, not far from the Nürburgring. It was during this time that Zakowski embraced a well-deserved retirement, reflecting on the eventful life he had led.

While Zakowski stepped back from the direct leadership of the Zakspeed racing team, his legacy continued through his sons, Peter and Philipp. They became integral figures in the motorsport world, carrying on the family’s dedication to excellence. The torch was passed to the next generation, ensuring that the Zakowski name would continue to be associated with motorsport success.
